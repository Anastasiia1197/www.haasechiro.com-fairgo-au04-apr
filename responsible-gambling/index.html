Healthy Habits and Strategies for Enjoyable Entertainment

Responsible gambling begins with awareness and proper preparation. Set limits in advance for both time and money you wish to spend on online entertainment. Always treat games as a form of entertainment, never as a way to earn money or recoup losses. Plan regular breaks during sessions and maintain balance with other hobbies and responsibilities in your life. These are the fundamental principles of healthy gaming habits.

Warning Signs of Problematic Gaming Behaviour

Early recognition of warning signs is crucial for maintaining control. Pay attention to signs such as: difficulty stopping play, feeling the need to gamble more to achieve the same excitement, lying to friends or family about time or money spent on gambling, financial problems or borrowing money to gamble, and neglecting work, studies, or social contacts. Do you recognise one or more of these signs in yourself or someone else? It's time to take action.

Debunking Common Myths About Gambling

There are persistent myths about gambling that can fuel risky behaviours. It is important to know them:

  • Online gambling is not an investment: The platform always has a statistical advantage in the long term. Gambling to make money is a dangerous mindset.
  • No "sure" strategy exists: No tactic or system can overcome the randomness (RNG) of fair slot games.
  • "Chasing losses" thinking: Losses cannot be recouped by playing longer or more; each round is independent. This concept is known as the "gambler's fallacy".

Support for Loved Ones and Families

Problem gambling has a significant impact not only on the gambler but also on their immediate surroundings. As a family member, partner, or friend, it is important to recognise the signs and know how to approach such a person. Open, non-judgmental communication is the first step. There are also specific support groups and helplines for loved ones in Australia.

Professional Support Organisations in Australia

If you notice that gambling is becoming a problem, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. In Australia 🇦🇺, there are several trustworthy organisations that offer free and anonymous help. For direct help and advice, you can turn to specialised institutions.

Examples of Self-Restriction Tools (on Platforms)

  • Daily, weekly, and monthly deposit limits
  • Time limits per session or per day
  • Self-exclusion for periods from 24 hours to permanent
  • Reality checks (notifications about playing time and spending)
  • Setting loss or win limits
  • Ability to set a break from play for a selected period

Important Support Organisations in Australia

  • Gambling Help Online (1800 858 858): Free, confidential 24/7 helpline and chat.
  • Gamblers Anonymous Australia: Organises support groups following the 12-step programme.
  • Lifeline Australia (13 11 14): Crisis support and mental health services.
  • Beyond Blue (1300 22 4636): Support for anxiety and depression related to gambling.
  • State-based gambling support services: Each state offers tailored help (e.g., Vic Responsible Gambling Foundation).
  • BetStop Australia: The national self-exclusion register for online wagering.
